Refract Labs

Where public knowledge change observability is useful.

Refract observes change in public knowledge repositories — Wikipedia, MediaWiki, and other revision-tracked sources. It surfaces what changed, when, and how, without claiming truth or accuracy. These labs explore where that signal matters outside healthcare.

AI Provenance

Stability, contestation, and recency signals for RAG pipelines and LLM citation trust.

Public Reference Monitoring

Track how brands, institutions, and public figures appear in Wikipedia — negative claims, controversy moves, source quality shifts.

Fandom Canon Intelligence

Canon disputes, retcon tracking, and source hierarchy conflicts in fandom wikis.

Legal Chronology

Public-record revision timelines for legal research and due diligence.

Enterprise Knowledge Governance

Internal knowledge base change tracking — separately governed from the open-source core.

Standards Intelligence

Track language changes in standards and specification documents.

BYO-Inference

Pipe Refract's deterministic event stream through an LLM of your choice at any audit-trailed boundary.

Experimental. Nothing here is a product commitment. These are research probes.
